Blockchains (& smart contracts) without cryptocurrency, in five tweets.

1/ Modern cross-border commercial deals require certainty as to project status and verifiability for stakeholders.

— Eris Industries (@eris_ltd) March 9, 2015

2/ These deals routinely outsize the market cap for the entire cryptocurrency field.

— Eris Industries (@eris_ltd) March 9, 2015

3/ Operating and/or tracking such deals on an openly committed (mined) blockchain only works when the deal value << system's market cap.

— Eris Industries (@eris_ltd) March 9, 2015

4/ Operating and/or tracking such deals with semi-public blockchains committed by identifiable entities, however, makes perfect sense.

— Eris Industries (@eris_ltd) March 9, 2015

5/ In such cases the legal realm is capable of providing the incentives necessary to hedge against bad actors within the system.

— Eris Industries (@eris_ltd) March 9, 2015
